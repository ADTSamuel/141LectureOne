Is there a minimum energy for seeing?


"The first measurements of the energy at the visual threshold were made by Langley (1889) with the bolometer he invented for such purposes. He found the energy to be $3\times 10^{-9}$ ergs light of 550 nm. Langley worked before the physiology of vision was understood, so that he used the wrong light and took none of the precautions now known to be necessary; even so, his results are too high only by a factor of 10."

-Hecht, Shlaer, and Pirenne

We have carefully studied all these accounts and have done our best to evaluate the measurements. Unfortunately, many of them contain serious errors which invalidate them. Probability of seeing curves

    

Let $a$ be the average number of quanta which a flash yields to the retina. Poisson statistics states that: \[ P_n = \frac{a^n}{n!} e^{-a} \]

If $\theta$ is the threshold for seeing, then the probability of seeing is a cumulative probability distribution: \[ P_{see} = \sum_{n=\theta}^{\infty} \frac{a^n}{n!} e^{-a} \]
