Sakitt's experiment

  • Dark-adapted humans were asked to rate blanks and dim flashes of light.

  • The ratings were 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6.

  • Stimuli were average flashes of 66 ("strong") or 55 ("weak") photons at the cornea.

    Quantum Optical Single Photon Light Source

  • Correlated pairs of photons (called signal and idler) are produced from a more energetic pump photon in a non-linear crystal following energy and momentum conservation.

  • By detecting one of the photons (idler) and sending the other (signal) to the observers eye, the setup creates an effective single-photon light source with sub-Poissonian photon number

    • Two-alternative Forced Choice

  • On the triggering of a trial, the subject was presented with two intervals, each heralded by a sound.
  • One of the two intervals was pseudorandomly chosen to trigger a possible single photon from SPDC around the peak rod sensitivity, while the other interval was a ‘blank’.
  • After the second interval the subject indicated which of the two intervals they thought contained the stimulus photons (interval response) and provided a confidence rating (R1–R3).

    • Single photon perception

  • Averaging across subjects’ responses and ratings from a total of 30,767 trials, 2,420 single-photon events passed post-selection.
  • Averaged probability of correct response was $0.516 \pm 0.010$ (P= 0.0545).

    • Subjects could detect a single photon with probability above chance.
